What is Physical Therapy?

Physical therapy (or physiotherapy) seeks to relieve pain, restore, maintain and promote optimal physical function as well as limit disabilities resulting from injury or disease.

Physical therapy utilizes a number of therapeutic methods, such as exercise therapy, manual therapy, electrotherapy also known as electrical physical agents (EPAs) such as laser, ultrasound, magnetic therapy, electrical stimulation as well as thermal agents of heat and cold. These agents are oftentimes referred to as modalities.

Physical Therapists

The Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT) degree is entry-level and the only PT degree offered by schools in the United States. Graduates have an option to go into a residency and then a fellowship.

As doctoral level direct access clinicians, we conduct an examination process that includes: taking your history, conducting a systems review, and performing tests and measures to identify the problem.

We will refer you to a different medical specialist if we determine that the problems to be addressed warrant such a referral.

If the problem falls within our scope, we establish a diagnosis (we tell you what the problem is) and a prognosis (we tell you how long it will take to resolve it).

Treatment

A comprehensive, tailored treatment plan is then created with a focus on your individual goals. Your therapist will utilize a range of treatment techniques to help you reach your goals.

Rehabilitation

Rehabilitation is a process which is dedicated to restoring, improving and maintaining physical strength and mobility, cognition and overall wellness. Rehabilitation involves specialists from many disciplines. We collaborate and communicate with many trusted and dedicated professionals in the area and we share a common goal of helping you achieve the highest level of function, independence, and quality of life possible.
