Why Us?

We are a team of orthopaedic Physical Therapists dedicated to quality care. We are privileged to be located in one office space with two orthopaedic surgeons and a Doctor of Podiatric Medicine with whom we cooperate very closely to ensure that your rehabilitative and therapeutic needs are fully met. We also have a professional relationship with doctors specializing in Pain Management. This ensures a comprehensive orthopaedic and rehabilitative care.

How are my problems diagnosed?

Diagnosis involves a careful, comprehensive assessment. This incorporates medical history, physical examination, and medical tests like MRIs, x-rays or ultrasound (depending on the patient and issue). Our doctor will be able to determine diagnosis and create a treatment plan. If required, we can recommend you to a specialist for additional examination and insight.

How long will my recovery take?

Acute low back pain or neck pain is usually treated very successfully within two or three sessions. Postoperative and chronic problems require a longer treatment time. Every case is different, and every person will respond to rehabilitation and treatment differently. You’ll have a much better understanding of your recovery timeframe after meeting with one of our doctors; establishing a clear diagnosis and relating it to your unique goals.

What are the steps involved in treatment ?

One common component of treatment is manual therapy, which is used to regain normal joint mobility and to release tight or overactive muscle and connective tissue. This is essential in recapturing optimal tissue flexibility and joint fluidity.

In addition, treatment commonly includes:

  • Dedicated rehabilitative exercises including postural corrective exercises.
  • Ergonomic/workplace condition assessment.
  • Newest and cutting edge equipment like laser, ultrasound, electrical stimulation.
  • Recommendations for ongoing improvement.

What can I do in the meantime if I’m suffering from pain or loss of function?

Avoid any activity that aggravates the affected area. Get in touch with your doctor or with one of our doctors as soon as possible to book a detailed assessment.
