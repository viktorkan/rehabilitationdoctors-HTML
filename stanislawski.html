Fellowship Trained in OMT

Dr. Thomas Stanislawski

DPT, FAAOMPT, Dip. Osteopractic Fellowship Trained in OMT

Dr. Thomas Stanislawski graduated from Long Island University, Brooklyn with a Doctorate degree in Physical Therapy where he also earned the Academic Excellence Award for his outstanding educational performance. Dr. Stanislawskiâ€™s unique style is influenced by several OMT (Orthopaedic Manual/Manipulative Therapy) systems he studied. This included but wasn't limited to:

1) OSTEOPRACTIC SYSTEM: This system is taught as a post-graduate, evidence based program conducted by the American Academy of Manipulative Therapy/Spinal Manipulation Institute/ Dry Needling Institute. After multiple written, oral and practical examinations and earning Certification in Dry Needling (Cert. DN), Certification in Spinal Manipulative Therapy (Cert. SMT), proving competent in the extremity thrust manipulation, usage of Instrument Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilization (IASTM) and meeting all other requirements of the Academy Dr. Stanislawski was awarded a Diploma in Osteopractic and became the first Osteopractor in New York State. Diploma in Osteopractic is available only to MD/DO/PT practitioners. It is not available to chiropractors.

2) NAIOMT SYSTEM: This system draws extensively on the work of James Cyriax, MD and the OMT Nordic System and is taught as a comprehensive, post-graduate manual therapy series organized by Andrews University, MI in cooperation with North American Institute of Orthopaedic Manual Therapy (NAIOMT). After completing all levels and demonstrating through written and practical exams a level of safety and competency Dr. Stanislawski was awarded a Certificate of Competence in Spinal Corrective Thrust Technique becoming the first such practitioner in New York State.

3) MULLIGAN CONCEPT: The Mulligan system of manual therapy is a complete system for treating the axial and appendicular joints using gentle mobilizing force. After completing all levels and demonstrating through written, oral and practical exams a level of safety and competency he was awarded a CMP - Certified Mulligan Practitioner title. The CMP title also attests to the proficiency in the usage of rigid tape and application of strapping techniques.

4) FELLOWSHIP in ORTHOPAEDIC MANUAL THERAPY ( AAMT/ University of South Carolina) - attainment of the Fellowship credential represents the highest level of proficiency and professionalism in OMT. Fellowship program develops and graduates practitioners, who by virtue of their critical thinking ability, clinical skills, diagnostic competence, and ethical standards are committed to promoting the optimum health and function of their patients, and who serve the profession through teaching, administration and research. Dr. Stanislawski mentors one Fellow-in-Training each year.
